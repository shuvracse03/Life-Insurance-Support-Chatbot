# Life Insurance Policy Types

## 1. Term Life Insurance

Term life insurance provides pure death-benefit coverage for a fixed period (term), such as 10, 20, or 30 years. If the insured dies within the term, the nominee receives the sum assured. There is no maturity benefit if the insured outlives the policy term.

**Key Features:**

- Lowest premiums among all life insurance types
- High sum assured for affordable premiums
- No savings or investment component
- Suitable for income replacement and liability coverage (e.g., home loans)

**Types of Term Plans:**

- Level Term: Sum assured remains constant throughout the term
- Increasing Term: Sum assured increases annually (good hedge against inflation)
- Decreasing Term: Sum assured decreases, often used to cover reducing liabilities like mortgages
- Return of Premium (TROP): Premiums are returned if the insured survives the term

---

## 2. Whole Life Insurance

Whole life insurance provides lifelong coverage (up to age 99 or 100). It combines a death benefit with a savings/cash-value component that grows over time.

**Key Features:**

- Lifelong protection with guaranteed death benefit
- Builds cash value that the policyholder can borrow against or withdraw
- Premiums are higher than term plans
- Suitable for estate planning and leaving a legacy

---

## 3. Endowment Plan

An endowment plan is a combination of life insurance and savings. It pays out the sum assured either on the policyholder's death during the term or on maturity if they survive.

**Key Features:**

- Dual benefit: protection + savings
- Guaranteed maturity benefit
- Lower returns compared to market-linked plans
- Suitable for goal-based saving (child's education, retirement)

---

## 4. Unit Linked Insurance Plan (ULIP)

A ULIP is a market-linked insurance product where a portion of the premium goes toward life cover and the remainder is invested in equity, debt, or balanced funds.

**Key Features:**

- Combines investment and insurance in one product
- Returns are market-linked (not guaranteed)
- Flexibility to switch between funds
- Lock-in period of 5 years
- Suitable for long-term wealth creation

---

## 5. Money-Back Policy

A money-back policy pays a percentage of the sum assured at regular intervals during the policy term (survival benefits), with the remaining sum paid at maturity or death.

**Key Features:**

- Periodic liquidity through survival benefits
- Full sum assured paid to nominee in case of death (regardless of survival benefits already paid)
- Lower overall returns due to periodic payouts
- Suitable for those who need periodic cash flow

---

## 6. Child Plans

Child insurance plans are designed to secure a child's future financial needs (education, marriage) even in the parent's absence.

**Key Features:**

- Waiver of premium benefit if the parent (policyholder) dies
- Policy continues and benefits are paid at maturity
- Can be term-based or ULIP-based

---

## 7. Retirement / Pension Plans

These plans help build a retirement corpus. The accumulated amount is used to purchase an annuity that provides regular income post-retirement.

**Key Features:**

- Accumulation phase: regular premium payments build corpus
- Vesting age: when the annuity/pension begins
- Options for lump-sum withdrawal or regular pension
