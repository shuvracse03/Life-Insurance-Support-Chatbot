# Life Insurance Benefits & Riders

## Core Benefits

### 1. Death Benefit

- The primary benefit of any life insurance policy
- Sum assured is paid to the nominee upon the policyholder's death during the policy term
- Can be paid as a lump sum, monthly income, or a combination of both

### 2. Maturity Benefit

- Available in endowment, money-back, and whole life plans
- The sum assured (plus bonuses, if applicable) is paid to the policyholder if they survive the policy term
- Term plans do NOT have a maturity benefit (except TROP plans)

### 3. Survival / Money-Back Benefit

- Periodic payouts (e.g., 20% of sum assured every 5 years) while the policy is active
- Applicable in money-back policies

### 4. Bonus (Participating Plans)

- **Reversionary Bonus:** Added to the sum assured each year; paid at claim
- **Terminal Bonus:** One-time bonus paid at maturity or death claim
- Only applicable to "with-profits" / participating plans

### 5. Tax Benefits

- **Section 80C:** Premiums up to ₹1.5 lakh per year are tax-deductible
- **Section 10(10D):** Death and maturity proceeds are generally tax-exempt
- **Section 80D:** Premiums for health riders are deductible up to ₹25,000

## Common Riders (Add-on Benefits)

### 1. Accidental Death Benefit Rider

- Pays an additional sum assured if death occurs due to an accident
- Example: Base sum ₹50L + Rider ₹50L = ₹1Cr paid on accidental death

### 2. Accidental Disability Rider

- Provides a lump sum or monthly income if the insured suffers permanent disability due to an accident
- Covers total and partial permanent disability

### 3. Critical Illness Rider

- Pays a lump sum on diagnosis of listed critical illnesses (cancer, heart attack, stroke, kidney failure, etc.)
- Typically covers 10–40 critical conditions depending on the insurer

### 4. Waiver of Premium Rider

- Waives all future premiums if the insured becomes permanently disabled or is diagnosed with a critical illness
- Policy continues with full benefits

### 5. Term Rider

- Adds extra life cover at a low additional premium
- Useful when temporary additional coverage is needed (e.g., during a loan repayment period)

### 6. Income Benefit Rider

- Pays a monthly income to the nominee for a fixed period after the policyholder's death
- Helps replace regular income for the family

### 7. Child Support Rider

- Provides a lump sum for the child's needs if the parent (policyholder) dies or becomes disabled

## Coverage Exclusions (Common)

- **Suicide:** Death by suicide within the first 12 months of policy issue is excluded (80% of premiums returned)
- **War and terrorism:** Deaths due to war, invasion, or act of terrorism may be excluded
- **Hazardous activities:** Skydiving, motor racing, mountaineering (unless declared and loaded)
- **Criminal acts:** Death while committing a criminal offence
- **Pre-existing illness:** Conditions not disclosed at the time of policy purchase

---

## Free-Look Period

- 15 days (30 days for policies sold via distance marketing) to review the policy
- If unsatisfied, the policyholder can return the policy for a full premium refund (minus stamp duty and medical examination costs)

## Grace Period

- 15 days for monthly premium payment mode
- 30 days for quarterly, half-yearly, or annual premium payment mode
- Policy remains active during the grace period; claim is valid if death occurs within grace period
