# Life Insurance Eligibility Criteria

## Age Eligibility

- **Minimum entry age:** 18 years (most policies)
- **Maximum entry age:** 65 years for term plans; 70 years for some whole life plans
- **Maximum maturity age:** 75–99 years depending on the policy type
- Child plans: entry age as low as 0–90 days for the insured child

## Health Requirements

- A medical examination may be required for high sum-assured policies or older applicants
- Common health assessments: blood pressure, blood sugar, ECG, BMI
- Pre-existing conditions (diabetes, hypertension, heart disease) may lead to:
  - Higher premiums (loading)
  - Exclusion of specific conditions from coverage
  - Policy rejection in severe cases
- Non-smoker discounts are available with most insurers

## Occupation

- Hazardous occupations (mining, defence, aviation, deep-sea fishing) may attract:
  - Higher premiums
  - Occupational exclusion riders
- Standard occupations (office work, teaching, IT) qualify for standard rates

## Financial Eligibility (Income-Based Sum Assured)

- Sum assured is typically limited to 10–20 times the annual income
- Insurers may request income proof (salary slips, ITR) for large sum-assured policies

## Residency & Citizenship

- Most Indian insurers require the policyholder to be an Indian resident
- NRIs can purchase term plans subject to specific conditions and documentation
- Foreign nationals residing in India may be eligible under special plans

## Documents Required for Purchase

- Identity proof: Aadhaar card, PAN card, Passport, Voter ID
- Address proof: Utility bill, bank statement, Aadhaar
- Age proof: Birth certificate, school certificate, Passport
- Income proof: Salary slips (last 3 months), ITR (last 2 years), Form 16
- Medical reports (if required based on age/sum assured)
- Recent passport-size photographs
