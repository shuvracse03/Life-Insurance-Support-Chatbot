# Life Insurance Claims Process

## Types of Claims

1. **Death Claim** – Filed by the nominee after the policyholder's death
2. **Maturity Claim** – Filed by the policyholder when the policy term ends
3. **Rider Claim** – Filed for additional benefits (e.g., accidental death rider, critical illness rider)

---

## Death Claim Process

### Step 1: Notify the Insurer

- Contact the insurance company immediately after the policyholder's death
- Notify via: toll-free helpline, email, insurer's website/portal, or branch office
- Provide: policy number, policyholder name, date of death

### Step 2: Obtain and Fill the Claim Form

- Download the Death Claim Form from the insurer's website or collect from the branch
- Fill accurately; any mismatch can delay processing

### Step 3: Gather Documents

**Mandatory documents:**

- Completed claim form (signed by nominee)
- Original policy bond / policy document
- Certified copy of Death Certificate (issued by municipal authority)
- Nominee's identity proof (Aadhaar, PAN, Passport, Voter ID)
- Nominee's address proof
- Cancelled cheque or bank passbook copy (for NEFT payment)
- NEFT mandate form

**Additional documents (based on cause of death):**

- **Natural / illness:** Doctor's certificate, hospital records, medical history
- **Accidental:** FIR copy, Post-mortem report, Panchnama, Police inquest report
- **Suicide (after 1 year of policy):** FIR, post-mortem report (subject to policy terms)

### Step 4: Submit Documents

- Submit to the nearest branch or upload via the online portal
- Get an acknowledgement receipt with a claim reference number

### Step 5: Claim Investigation

- For early claims (death within 3 years of policy issue), the insurer may conduct a field investigation
- Typical investigation timeline: 7–15 working days

### Step 6: Claim Settlement

- IRDAI mandate: Insurers must settle claims within **30 days** of receiving all documents
- If investigation is needed: within **90 days**
- Payment is made via NEFT directly to the nominee's bank account

---

## Maturity Claim Process

1. Insurer sends advance intimation ~1 month before maturity date
2. Policyholder submits: original policy document, identity proof, bank details, discharge voucher
3. Payment is made on or before the maturity date

---

## Claim Rejection Reasons

- Non-disclosure of pre-existing illness at policy inception
- Death due to excluded causes (suicide within first year, war, criminal acts)
- Lapsed policy (premium not paid, grace period expired)
- Fraudulent documentation
- Claim filed after the stipulated time limit

---

## Claim Escalation

- If unsatisfied: escalate to the insurer's Grievance Redressal Officer
- Further escalation: Insurance Ombudsman (free of cost, for claims up to ₹50 lakhs)
- Final recourse: Consumer Forum / IRDAI complaint portal (https://bimabharosa.irdai.gov.in)
